About Joe

From Brigade to Barbell

I’m Joe – a restaurant chef who fell in love with lifting weights and tracking macros, then realised the food in most “fitness” plans looked like punishment. This is the bit where the two worlds finally shake hands.

I grew up in proper hotel kitchens: white jackets, hot passes, and the kind of standards that make you remake a sauce because it’s one shade too pale. I’ve worked across UK hotels and restaurants, and was part of the team that helped Coworth Park earn a Michelin star – the sort of environment where every plate has to be worth the walk from the table to the kitchen if a guest sends it back.

Somewhere between services I discovered the gym. What started as “try not to snap my back on deadlifts” slowly turned into logging macros, chasing progressive overload and seeing food as fuel as much as flavour.

The problem? Most of the food aimed at lifters looked like it had been cooked by someone who hated joy – dry chicken, grey rice, broccoli that had seen things. I knew there was a better way to do it, using the same discipline and technique we use on a restaurant pass.

The idea behind Fit Food Chef UK

Fit Food Chef UK is where I take the rules of restaurant cooking – good sourcing, proper seasoning, controlled heat, clean plates – and apply them to food that still respects your calories and protein target.

I’m not here to pretend a triple-cooked chip is a macro-friendly vegetable. I am here to show you how to build a plate that hits your numbers and makes you excited to eat it, instead of just getting it over with before bed.

Some days that’s a 700-calorie high-protein burger with a stupidly shiny glaze. Other days it’s a lower-fat, higher-volume dinner that stops you raiding the snack cupboard at 10pm. The point is: flavour first, numbers respected.

What you’ll get from me

Expect recipes that look like they belong in a restaurant, written for real people who also happen to care about building muscle, losing fat or just not feeling like a zombie at the gym.

I’ll talk about things like resting meat properly, why your potatoes never roast quite right, how to build sauces that don’t taste like “diet food”, and how to tweak traditional dishes so they fit your macros without crying over what’s missing.

If you like the sound of chef-level food that still respects your calorie budget – with a bit of British sarcasm thrown in – you’re in the right place. Stick around, cook a few things, and let me know how it changes your meal prep.
